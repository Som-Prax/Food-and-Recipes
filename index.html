1. Yong Tau Foo

Chinese Hakka
Traditional
Meat & Vegetables

Description

Yong tau foo (Chinese: 酿豆腐) is a Hakka Chinese dish consisting primarily of tofu filled with ground meat mixture or fish paste. Variation of this food include vegetables and mushrooms stuffed with ground meat or surimi. Yong tau foo is eaten in numerous ways, either dry with a sauce or served as a soup dish.

Recipe

Ingredients: Oil, Water, Salt, Pepper, 1kg Fresh Mackerel, Chicken, 2tablespoon Tapioca Flour, Chili, Toufu, and Engplant.

  1. Slit the fish and scoop out fish flesh with a spoon.
  2. Mince chicken till smooth.
  3. Put fish flesh into a blender to blend.
  4. Add in minced chicken, tapioca flour, salt, and pepper and blend well.
  5. Add in water until it becomes sticky and smooth.
  6. Lift the paste and give it a couple of snaps to yield a better texture.
  7. Cut the chilli and eggplant.
  8. Put fish paste into chilli, eggplant, and toufu.
  9. Fried until golden brown and serve. Remove and drain the oil.
  10. Serve hot.

2. Escargot

French
Delicate
Bizarre

Description

Escargot, (French: “snail”) any of several species of edible land snails, a delicacy of French cuisine. In France, where escargots are a popular dish, they are usually served as light appetizers. Like other seafood, however, escargots can be eaten in different forms—baked into a tart, for instance, or served on a roll like lobster.

Recipe

Ingredients: Canned snails, unsalted butter, breadcrumbs, shallot, parsley and garlic.

  1. Melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a pan, then add shallot and sauté until they soften. Lastly add snails for about 2 minutes.
  2. Add 5 tablespoon of butter, garlic, and parsley in a blender. Pulse under it all comes together to make a garlic butter.
  3. Place one snail in each escargot compartment and cover with about half a tablespoon or so of the garlic butter mixture. Then, sprinkle each snail with a little bit of breadcrumbs.
  4. Bake the snails at 375 degrees for 10-15 minutes until they brown.
  5. Serve the escargots with baguette toast.

3. Chili Pan Mee (Noodles)

Hot
Cantonese
Carbohydrates

Description

Pan Mee, (Chinese: 板面) is a popular Chinese noodle dish, consisting of handmade noodles served in soup. It consists of egg noodles served in a flavorful soup, often with some type of meat or fish, vegetables and various spices. Dried anchovies, minced pork, mushrooms, and leafy vegetables are also possible ingredients.

Recipe

Ingredients: Oil, dried chilis, dried shrimp, mushrooms, minced chicken, spinach, eggs, anchovies.

  1. Soak the dried chilies, dried shrimp, and mushrooms separately in warm water until they are soft.
  2. Put the chili, dried shrimp, and garlic in a food processor and chop it finely.
  3. Add oil to a preheat pan. Transfer the chili mixture to the pan and stir fry over medium heat until they are dry and the color turns dark, but not burnt, this may take 20-30 minutes. Remove from the heat and set aside.
  4. Preheat the oil over medium heat and fry the anchovies until they are crispy. Remove the anchovies and place on absorbent paper towel.
  5. Keep the oil to cook the meat topping.
  6. ring the oil back to hot over medium heat. Stir fry the minced chicken until they turn opaque in color.
  7. Add the mushrooms and let it gently simmer until the meat is cooked through and the liquid reduces a bit. Adjust seasonings to your taste
  8. Boil spinach.
  9. Serve hot with chili paste, ground meat, mushrooms, spinach, poached eggs, and crispy anchovies.

4. Singaporean Noodles

Singaporean
Diverse
Carbohydrates

Description

Singapore-style noodles, (Chinese: 星洲炒米) is a dish of stir-fried cooked rice vermicelli, curry powder, vegetables, scrambled eggs and meat, most commonly chicken or prawns. Singapore noodles are a Cantonese creation, and are very common in Cantonese-style and takeaway restaurants in Hong Kong.

Recipe

Ingredients: Oil, prawns, thin noodles, onions, bell peppers, carrots, soy sauce, curry powder, egg.

  1. Peel the prawns, and marinate them in oil and fish sauce.
  2. Cut the onions, peppers and carrots.
  3. Scramble an egg and set aside.
  4. Fry the prawns with onion, bell peppers and carrots.
  5. Season with curry powder and set aside.
  6. Cook the thin noodles with a bit of oil.
  7. Add soy sauce, curry powder and salt to the noodles.
  8. Add all other ingredients and stir fry.
  9. Serve hot.

5. Lei Cha

Vegetarian
Chinese Hakka
Healthy

Description

Lei cha, (Chinese: 擂茶) or ground tea is a traditional Southern Chinese tea-based beverage or gruel that forms a part of Hakka cuisine. It is often served with rice, tofu, peanuts and vegetables.

Recipe

Ingredients: Lei Cha powder (or you can grind them yourself), white rice, peanuts, tofu, soybeans, and other vegetables.

  1. Add water to Lei Cha powder to make it diluted.
  2. Stir fry tofu until it is slightly brown.
  3. Sitr fry vegetables.
  4. Stir fry peanuts until it is crispy.
  5. Place rice with tofu, peanuts, and other vegetables in a big bowl.
  6. Pour Lei Cha paste into the bowl.
  7. Serve hot.
